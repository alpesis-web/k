Unbalanced Coin

Jul 29th, 2014 10:54 pm | Comments

If there is an unbalanced coin, the probability of the head is p (0\<\p\<1), while of the tail is 1-p, how can you get the same probability of this coin?

To flip the coin twice,

  • 1. prob(head, head) = p * p
  • 2. prob(head, tail) = p * (1-p)
  • 3. prob(tail, head) = (1-p) * p
  • 4. prob(tail, tail) = (1-p) * (1-p)

If the situation 1 & 4, flip the coin twice again.
